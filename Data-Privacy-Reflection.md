# Data Privacy & Confidentiality Reflection

## Key Takeaways from Focus Bear's Privacy Policy

Focus Bear Pty Ltd (ABN 59 659 843 964) operates as the **data Controller** under the GDPR (EU) 2016/679. The policy governs how personal data is collected, used, disclosed, and protected across their website and apps.

Key principles include:
- Transparency about what data is collected and why
- Legal bases for processing personal data (consent, legitimate interest, contractual necessity)
- User rights under GDPR (access, correction, deletion, portability)
- Defined data retention periods and security measures to preserve confidentiality, integrity, and availability
- A dedicated privacy contact: **privacy@focusbear.io**

## What Types of Data Are Considered Confidential at Focus Bear?

Based on Focus Bear's privacy policy and general data handling best practices, confidential data includes:

- **Personal user data** — names, email addresses, account credentials, and usage behaviour collected via the app and website
- **Behavioural/productivity data** — focus session logs, app usage patterns, habit tracking data inputted by users
- **Business/company data** — internal strategies, product roadmaps, unreleased features, financial information, and partner agreements
- **Employee and team data** — HR records, performance information, internal communications
- **Technical credentials** — API keys, access tokens, database passwords, and environment secrets

## Best Practices for Handling Confidential Data

1. **Access control** — Only access data you need for your specific role (principle of least privilege)
2. **Secure storage** — Store sensitive data in approved, encrypted systems; avoid local storage on personal devices
3. **Strong authentication** — Use unique, strong passwords and enable MFA on all work accounts
4. **Encrypted communication** — Share sensitive information only through approved, encrypted channels (not personal email or unencrypted messaging)
5. **Data minimisation** — Collect and retain only what is necessary; avoid copying sensitive data unnecessarily
6. **Clean desk / clear screen** — Lock screens when away and avoid leaving sensitive data visible
7. **Careful sharing** — Double-check recipients before sending data; avoid CC-ing unnecessary parties

## How to Respond to a Suspected Data Breach or Accidental Disclosure

If I suspect or discover a data breach or accidental disclosure, I will:

1. **Act immediately** — Do not delay; time matters in limiting damage
2. **Do not attempt to fix it alone** — Escalate to my manager and/or the relevant security/privacy contact
3. **Report internally** — Notify the appropriate team lead and contact **privacy@focusbear.io** as per Focus Bear's policy
4. **Document the incident** — Record what happened, when, what data may have been exposed, and who may be affected
5. **Preserve evidence** — Avoid deleting anything that may be needed for investigation
6. **Cooperate with the response process** — Follow the incident response plan and provide full transparency

> Under GDPR, data breaches that risk individuals' rights must be reported to the relevant supervisory authority within **72 hours**.

## Reflection

### Steps I Can Take to Handle Data Securely Day-to-Day
- Always lock my screen when stepping away from my computer
- Use a password manager to maintain strong, unique credentials for all work accounts
- Avoid storing sensitive information in personal cloud storage or email drafts
- Review permissions when accessing shared drives — only keep access I actively need
- Think before sharing: ask "does this person need this information, and is this channel appropriate?"

### Storing, Sharing, and Disposing of Sensitive Information
- **Store:** Use company-approved tools with encryption at rest; avoid local copies
- **Share:** Use secure, access-controlled platforms; confirm recipient identity before sending
- **Dispose:** Properly delete/shred documents; use data deletion tools rather than simply moving to trash

### Common Mistakes That Lead to Data Privacy Issues
| Mistake | How to Avoid |
|---|---|
| Sending data to the wrong recipient | Double-check email/message recipients before sending |
| Using weak or reused passwords | Use a password manager and enable MFA |
| Storing data in unapproved locations (e.g. personal Google Drive) | Always use company-approved storage |
| Sharing credentials verbally or via chat | Use secure credential-sharing tools; never share passwords in plaintext |
| Leaving sessions logged in on shared devices | Always log out and lock screens |
| Ignoring software update prompts | Keep all software patched and up to date |

## Habit / Practice to Adopt

**Habit:** Before sharing any file or message containing sensitive information, I will pause and apply a quick **"STOP" check**:
- **S**ensitive? — Does this contain personal, financial, or confidential data?
- **T**ransfer secure? — Am I using an approved, encrypted channel?
- **O**nly the right people? — Have I verified all recipients truly need this?
- **P**ermission confirmed? — Do I have authorisation to share this data?

## Key Learning & Security Measure I Will Implement

**Learning:** Data privacy is not just a legal obligation — it is a trust commitment to every Focus Bear user. Mishandling even a small amount of user data can erode trust, cause harm, and create legal consequences under GDPR.

**Security Measure I Will Implement:** Enable and consistently use **Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)** on all work-related accounts. MFA is one of the most effective single steps to prevent unauthorised access, even if a password is compromised.
