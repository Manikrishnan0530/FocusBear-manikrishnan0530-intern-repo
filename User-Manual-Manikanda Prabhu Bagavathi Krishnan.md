## How I Work Best

**I do my best work when...**
I have a long, uninterrupted deep-work block. ML work — whether it's running experiments, debugging a pipeline, or reading a paper — requires sustained focus. I am most productive when I can get into a flow state without being pulled in multiple directions.

**I struggle when...**
There are too many interruptions or context switches. Jumping between unrelated tasks or conversations mid-focus is the quickest way to derail my thinking. If something can wait, async is always appreciated over an instant interruption.

## Communication Style

**I prefer messages to be...**
Async — send me a message on Discord, Slack, or email and I will respond thoughtfully when I am at a good stopping point. I am not one to leave things unanswered; I just work better when I can respond on my own terms rather than reactively.

**The best way to reach me is...**
Discord or Slack for day-to-day communication. Email works too for anything more formal or detailed.

**If you need something urgent, do this...**
Call or text me directly. Async messages can sit unread during a deep-work block, so if it genuinely can't wait, a call is the clearest signal that something needs immediate attention.

## Collaboration & Teamwork

**I work best in a team when...**
There's psychological safety to ask questions freely. I'm still learning a lot, and being in an environment where curiosity is welcomed — not judged — makes a huge difference to the quality of my work and how comfortable I am contributing ideas.

**My biggest pet peeve in teamwork is...**
Ambiguity that could easily have been resolved upfront. Clear goals and expectations set everyone up to collaborate better.

**If I seem quiet in a meeting, it usually means...**
I am listening carefully and processing. I tend to think before I speak, so silence from me is not disengagement — I will contribute when I have something worth saying.

## Focus & Productivity

**I stay focused by...**
Blocking out distractions deliberately — silencing notifications, putting my phone away, and protecting chunks of uninterrupted time for deep work. Having a clear task to work on at the start of a session helps a lot too.

**Things that distract me...**
Noise, social media, and unrelated notifications are the main culprits. I do best in a calm, low-stimulation environment when I need to think deeply.

**The best way to support my productivity is...**
Respect my focus blocks where possible. If a message is not urgent, async is always the better default. Knowing I won't be pulled into unexpected meetings mid-task lets me commit fully to what I am working on.

## Feedback & Growth

**I like feedback to be...**
Direct and specific. Tell me exactly what to improve and why — I can handle honesty, and vague or sugar-coated feedback is harder to act on. Concrete examples are especially helpful.

**When I make a mistake, the best way to let me know is...**
Point it out clearly and privately. I'd rather know and fix it than have something go unaddressed. I won't be defensive — I genuinely want to improve.

**I appreciate it when teammates...**
