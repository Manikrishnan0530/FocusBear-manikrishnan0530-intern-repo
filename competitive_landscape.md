## 1. What makes Focus Bear different from these apps?
What makes Focus Bear different from these apps is that it combines multiple features into one system instead of focusing on just one thing. Apps like Freedom and Cold Turkey mainly block distractions, while Tiimo and Routinery focus more on routines and scheduling.

Focus Bear brings all of these together by helping users build routines, stay focused, and avoid distractions at the same time. It is also designed to support neurodivergent users, which makes it more helpful compared to general productivity apps.

## 2. If you were a user, why would you choose Focus Bear over competitors?
If I were a user, I would choose Focus Bear because it provides multiple features in a single app instead of relying on different tools for different purposes. I prefer using one system that handles focus, routines, and distraction control together, as it reduces the need to switch between apps.

Using multiple apps can become confusing and inconsistent over time, especially if they do not integrate well. Focus Bear feels more structured and reliable as everything is managed in one place.

## 3. What’s one feature that other apps have that Focus Bear doesn’t?
One feature that some other apps do better is strict and reliable app blocking. While using Focus Bear, I noticed that even after enabling app blocking, I was still able to access apps like Instagram.

In comparison, apps like Cold Turkey are known for enforcing stronger restrictions that are harder to bypass. This makes them more effective for users who need strict control over distractions.

## 4. Based on your research, what’s one improvement you think Focus Bear could make?
One improvement Focus Bear could make is to strengthen its app blocking system. Based on my experience, the blocking should be more strict and harder to bypass once it is enabled by the user.

Additionally, Focus Bear could improve cross-device support by allowing users to grant permissions to block apps across different platforms such as mobile, laptop, and iPad. This would make the blocking system more consistent and reliable, regardless of the device being used.

Overall, improving the reliability and enforcement of app blocking would make the app more effective for users who need strong control over distractions.
